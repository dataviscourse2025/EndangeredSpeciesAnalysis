Historical Endangered Species Analysis

By Sydney Lundberg and Victoria Mache

Before and Early ESA Era

Scroll to explore how the total number of listed endangered species changes over time and how major amendments to the Endangered Species Act shaped that trend.

1978

A federal “God Squad” committee is created to allow exemptions for projects that would otherwise jeopardize listed species, and economics enter habitat decisions.

1982

Listing decisions must be based only on science, with deadlines added. Experimental populations and Habitat Conservation Plans are introduced.

1988

Monitoring requirements and recovery plans are strengthened, and tracking continues even after species are delisted.

2004

The Department of Defense can be exempt from some critical habitat restrictions if it has an approved natural resources management plan.

Stepping Back

Together, these policy changes help explain why the curve of endangered species listings looks the way it does,by increasing attention, changing rules, and evolving conservation priorities.
